Cooking tips for chicken

  • Brown chicken first; the fond becomes the base of your sauce.
  • Thighs stay juicier than breasts in long cooks; breasts suit quick sautés.
  • Season in layers — once on raw chicken, again when building the sauce.
  • Deglaze the pan before adding liquid to pick up browned bits.
  • Check doneness with a thermometer — 165°F/74°C in the thickest part.
